Model Y

The Tesla Model Y is an electric compact crossover utility vehicle (CUV) by Tesla, Inc. Tesla unveiled it in March 2019,[2] started production at its Fremont plant in January 2020[3] and started deliveries on March 13, 2020.

It is the second vehicle based on the Model 3 sedan platform.[5] Model Y will offer optional third-row seats for a seven-passenger seating capacity,[6][7][8] currently planned for Q4 2020.

There were four planned powertrain configurations for the Model Y: Standard Range, Long Range, Long Range with Dual-Motor All-Wheel Drive, and Performance. The Long Range AWD model and the Performance model are currently being delivered.[11] The Standard Range RWD power train option was dropped from the model line up in early July 2020, due to the range being unacceptably low to Musk.

The Model Y fills a smaller size segment from its bigger sibling, the mid-sized Tesla Model X.[13] The Model Y shares an estimated 75% of its part with the Tesla Model 3,[14] which includes a similar interior design and electric powertrain.

History

In 2013, Tesla Motors filed for a trademark on "Model Y".

In 2015, Elon Musk teased a Model 3-based Model Y with falcon-wing doors.

In 2017, the Model Y's silhouette was teased to Tesla shareholders at the annual general meeting in June.[18] Elon Musk also announced that the Model Y would be produced in a new factory, as it was not likely that the Fremont plant would have room to accommodate another production line.
